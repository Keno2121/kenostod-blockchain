The Proof-of-Residual-Value (PoRV) Consensus and Monetization Model
This model integrates your blockchain's security mechanism directly with a high-value, recurring commercial service, ensuring that the network's core function is its primary revenue driver.
1. The Unique Feature: Proof-of-Residual-Value (PoRV)
PoRV is an evolution of Proof-of-Useful-Work (PoUW) that ties the network's security and block rewards not just to performing a useful computation (like training an AI model), but to the ongoing commercial value generated by that computation.
How PoRV Works on Kenostod:
High-Value Work (Immediate Return): Kenostod miners (validators) are required to use their computational power (GPUs/CPUs) to perform commercially valuable tasks, such as:
Training proprietary AI/Machine Learning models for enterprise clients.
Running complex scientific simulations (e.g., drug discovery, climate modeling).
Processing large-scale data analytics for market research.
The Residual Link (Long-Term Residual Income): This is the core innovation. When a block is successfully validated, the miner is rewarded with the native Kenostod token (let's call it $KNO). However, the smart contract governing the block reward also creates a Residual Value Token (RVT) that is permanently linked to the specific computational output (e.g., the trained AI model).
Monetization (Obsolete Future): The firm owning Kenostod licenses the final computational product (the AI model, the simulation data) to external clients.
Immediate High Return: The client pays a large upfront licensing fee in $KNO or a stablecoin. This fee is immediately distributed to the firm and the network treasury.
Long-Term Residual Income: Crucially, the licensing agreement is structured as a perpetual revenue share. Every time the client uses the licensed product (e.g., every API call to the AI model, every sale of a drug discovered by the simulation), a small, fractional royalty fee is automatically collected by a smart contract.
The Obsolete Factor: A percentage of this royalty fee is used to perpetually buy and burn the $KNO token, and another percentage is distributed directly to the wallets holding the original RVTs. This ties the long-term value of the token and the miner's residual income to the success of the commercial products built on the chain. This mechanism makes standard staking/transaction-fee models obsolete, as it turns network security into a perpetual, revenue-sharing business.
