## 3. Customer Identification Program (CIP)

### 3.1 Identity Verification Requirements

All users must complete identity verification before:
- Withdrawing KENO tokens to external wallets
- Converting KENO to USD
- Receiving ACH transfers

### 3.2 Required Information

**Individual Users:**
- Full legal name
- Date of birth
- Residential address
- Government-issued ID number (SSN, passport, or driver's license)
- Email address
- Phone number

**Business Entities:**
- Legal business name
- Business address
- EIN/Tax ID
- Formation documents
- Beneficial ownership information (25%+ owners)

### 3.3 Verification Methods

**Documentary Verification:**
- Government-issued photo ID (passport, driver's license, state ID)
- Utility bill or bank statement (address verification)
- Business registration documents (for entities)

**Non-Documentary Verification:**
- Third-party identity verification services
- Database verification (credit bureaus, public records)
- Cross-referencing provided information

### 3.4 Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD)

Enhanced verification is required for:
- Transactions exceeding $3,000 in a single day
- Users from high-risk jurisdictions
- Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs)
- Users flagged for suspicious activity
- Cash-out requests exceeding $10,000 cumulative

## 4. Know Your Customer (KYC) Procedures

### 4.1 KYC Tiers

**Tier 1 - Basic (Course Completion Only)**
- Email verification
- Platform account creation
- No USD withdrawal privileges

**Tier 2 - Standard (USD Withdrawals up to $1,000/month)**

**Tier 3 - Enhanced (USD Withdrawals above $1,000/month)**

- Enhanced identity verification
- Proof of course completion records

### 4.2 Ongoing Monitoring

- Transaction monitoring for unusual patterns
- Periodic re-verification (annually)
- Sanctions screening updates
- Risk profile reassessment

## 5. Transaction Monitoring

### 5.1 Monitoring Procedures

The Company monitors all transactions for:
- Unusual transaction patterns
- Rapid movement of funds
- Transactions inconsistent with user profile
- Structuring to avoid reporting thresholds
- Transactions involving sanctioned parties

### 5.2 Red Flags

Suspicious indicators include:
- Multiple accounts from same identity
- Rapid course completion followed by immediate withdrawal
- Transactions just below $10,000 threshold
- Inconsistent personal information
- Refusal to provide required documentation
- Transactions involving high-risk jurisdictions
- Use of VPNs or anonymizing services

### 5.3 Transaction Limits

| User Tier | Daily Limit | Monthly Limit |
|-----------|-------------|---------------|
| Tier 1    | $0 (no USD) | $0 (no USD)   |
| Tier 2    | $500        | $1,000        |
| Tier 3    | $5,000      | $25,000       |

## 6. Suspicious Activity Reporting (SAR)

### 6.1 SAR Filing Requirements

A SAR must be filed when:
- Transactions of $2,000+ involve suspected money laundering
- Transactions of $5,000+ involve suspected criminal activity
- Any transaction involving potential terrorist financing
- Patterns suggesting structuring or smurfing

### 6.2 Filing Procedures

1. Compliance Officer reviews flagged transactions within 24 hours
2. Investigation documented in internal case file
3. SAR filed via BSA E-Filing within 30 days of detection
4. Records retained for 5 years from filing date

### 6.3 Confidentiality

- SARs are confidential and not disclosed to subjects
- Staff prohibited from "tipping off" investigated parties
- SAR filing records secured separately from general files

---

## 7. Currency Transaction Reports (CTR)

### 7.1 CTR Filing Requirements

A CTR is required for:
- Cash transactions exceeding $10,000 in a single business day
- Multiple transactions by same person totaling over $10,000

### 7.2 Filing Procedures

1. CTR filed via BSA E-Filing within 15 days
2. Customer notified of CTR filing requirement
3. Records retained for 5 years

---

## 8. OFAC Sanctions Compliance

### 8.1 Screening Requirements

All users are screened against:
- OFAC Specially Designated Nationals (SDN) List
- OFAC Consolidated Sanctions List
- Other applicable sanctions lists

### 8.2 Screening Frequency

- Initial screening at account creation
- Re-screening at each KYC tier upgrade
- Daily batch screening of active users
- Real-time screening for transactions over $1,000

### 8.3 Match Handling

1. Potential matches escalated to Compliance Officer
2. Investigation within 24 hours
3. Account frozen pending resolution
4. True matches reported to OFAC
5. False positives documented and cleared
