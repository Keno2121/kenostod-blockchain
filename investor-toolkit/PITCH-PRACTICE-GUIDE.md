# 🎤 INVESTOR PITCH PRACTICE GUIDE

**How to Deliver a Killer 20-Minute Presentation**

---

## 🎯 PITCH STRUCTURE OVERVIEW

**Total Time:** 20 minutes presentation + 10 minutes Q&A = 30 minutes

**Energy Flow:**
- Start STRONG (grab attention)
- Build URGENCY (the problem)
- Show SOLUTION (your platform)
- Prove TRACTION (what you've built)
- Close with THE ASK (clear investment terms)

---

## 📝 SLIDE-BY-SLIDE TALKING POINTS

## 🎯 HANDLING Q&A (10 minutes)

### **COMMON QUESTIONS & ANSWERS:**

**Q: "What's your customer acquisition cost?"**
> "We're targeting $20-30 per student through social media ads in Africa and Brazil, where CPMs are much lower than the US. We've budgeted $900K for marketing in Year 1, which should get us 30-45K new students. Our LTV is $180-240 per student (12-16 months average), so we have a healthy 6-8X LTV to CAC ratio."

**Q: "How do you prevent students from just cashing out KENO immediately?"**
> "Great question. We have a 30-day vesting period for earned tokens, and we incentivize holding with our Graduate Club system—students who hold their KENO and complete all courses get exclusive benefits like job referrals, premium courses, and higher earning rates. Plus, as the platform grows and more students want to buy KENO to participate, there's natural demand."

**Q: "What if KENO price crashes?"**
> "Our business model doesn't depend on KENO price. We have 7 revenue streams, and only the token sales (ICO) are price-dependent. Student subscriptions, B2B revenue, transaction fees—all are in USD. If KENO price drops, students earn more tokens per lesson, which actually increases their incentive to learn. We're not a speculative token project; we're an education business."

**Q: "Why not just use Bitcoin or Ethereum instead of creating your own token?"**
> "Three reasons: First, we need granular control over token economics—reward rates, vesting schedules, student incentives. Second, we need low transaction fees for microtransactions (students earning small amounts per lesson), which is why we chose BSC. Third, having our own token creates a complete ecosystem—students earn it, trade it, use it for premium features, and eventually it becomes tradeable on exchanges."

**Q: "How are you different from Coinbase Earn?"**
> "Coinbase Earn gives you $3 for watching a 5-minute video. We're giving students comprehensive blockchain education—21 full courses—plus career placement. Coinbase Earn is a marketing gimmick to onboard users. We're building a complete educational institution that happens to reward students with crypto. Totally different value proposition."

**Q: "What's to stop a competitor from copying you?"**
> "First-mover advantage in emerging markets is huge. We're already live with 21 courses, verified token, B2B clients. It would take a competitor 12-18 months to build what we have. By then, we'll have 50,000 students and strong brand recognition in Africa, Brazil, and India. Plus, we have network effects—more students = more job placements = more students. And we're building relationships with universities and corporates that will license our platform. Moats are being built every day."

**Q: "Why should I invest in Private Sale vs. waiting for Public Sale or exchange listing?"**
> "Three reasons: One, you get the 20% bonus—that's only available in Private Sale. Two, you're getting in at one cent vs. five cents in Public Sale—that's 5X right there. Three, you're taking the least risk because you can see the working product right now at kenostodblockchain.com. By Public Sale, you're paying 5X more for the same thing. By exchange listing, you might pay 10-20X more and you're competing with retail FOMO buyers."

**Q: "What are the regulatory risks?"**
> "We've designed around the biggest risk—US securities laws—by explicitly excluding US and Canadian investors. For our target markets (Africa, Brazil, India, SE Asia), regulations are much more favorable to crypto. We're also not promising profits from our efforts alone; students earn by completing courses, not by holding tokens. And we have legal counsel advising us on compliance in each market. This is not a 'move fast and break things' approach; we're being very deliberate about compliance."

**Q: "How do you plan to get listed on exchanges?"**
> "We're in conversations with three tier-2 centralized exchanges (KuCoin, Gate.io, MEXC) and planning for Q1 2026 listings. Listing costs are typically $50K-150K, which we've budgeted in our use of funds. We're also building liquidity on PancakeSwap (BSC's biggest DEX) to enable immediate trading. The key is we don't NEED exchange listings for the business to work—they're just upside for token holders."

## 🎭 DELIVERY TIPS

### **VOICE & PACE:**
- Speak at 140-160 words per minute (conversational, not rushed)
- Pause after big numbers or key points (3-5 seconds)
- Vary your tone: excited for vision, serious for problem, confident for solution
- Project your voice, don't mumble

### **BODY LANGUAGE:**
- Stand tall, shoulders back
- Make eye contact (if in-person) or look at camera (if virtual)
- Use hand gestures to emphasize points
- Smile when appropriate (especially on "earn crypto while learning")
- Don't fidget, don't pace nervously

### **ENERGY MANAGEMENT:**
- Start at 70% energy (confident but not overwhelming)
- Ramp up to 90% during "The Solution" slide
- Stay at 80-85% through financials and traction
- Peak at 95% on "The Ask"
- End at 85% with warm smile

### **COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID:**
- ❌ Reading slides word-for-word
- ❌ Talking too fast (nervousness)
- ❌ Apologizing ("Sorry, this slide is a bit busy")
- ❌ Going over time (respect their schedule)
- ❌ Being defensive during Q&A
- ❌ Badmouthing competitors
- ❌ Overselling ("This will 100% 10X in 6 months")
- ❌ Not knowing your numbers

## 📊 PRACTICE CHECKLIST

**Before your first real pitch, do this:**

- [ ] Practice the full pitch 10 times alone
- [ ] Record yourself on video and watch it
- [ ] Practice in front of a friend and get feedback
- [ ] Time yourself (should be 18-22 minutes)
- [ ] Memorize the key numbers (10K students Y1, $2M revenue Y1, etc.)
- [ ] Prepare answers to 10 common questions
- [ ] Test all technology (screen share, remote, etc.)
- [ ] Have backup plan if tech fails (printout, PDF, etc.)
- [ ] Get a good night's sleep before pitch day
- [ ] Dress professionally (even on video calls)

## 🎯 FINAL TIPS FOR SUCCESS

**The night before:**
- Review your deck one last time
- Get 8 hours of sleep
- Lay out your outfit
- Test your tech setup

**The day of:**
- Eat a light meal (not too heavy)
- Arrive/login 10 minutes early
- Do vocal warmups if presenting in-person
- Take 3 deep breaths before starting
- Smile and project confidence

**During the pitch:**
- Be yourself (authentic beats polished)
- Show passion for the mission
- Back up claims with data
- Welcome questions, don't fear them
- End with clear next steps

**After the pitch:**
- Send thank you email within 24 hours
- Attach deck and one-pager
- Propose specific next steps (call, diligence, etc.)
- Follow up in 3-5 days if no response
- Log everything in your tracker
