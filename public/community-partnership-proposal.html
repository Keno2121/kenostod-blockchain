Who We Are

Kenostod Blockchain Academy is an Education-Finance (Education-Fi) platform built under the T.D.I.R. Foundation — Turn Dreams Into Reality. Our mission is financial sovereignty for communities that have historically been excluded from economic systems.

We teach people how money really works: how to build and protect wealth, how to use digital financial tools that do not require a credit score or a bank's approval, and how to participate in the modern digital economy. Every course is designed to be accessible to someone with zero prior financial or technology experience.

2

The 4-Week Pilot Program

We are offering a free, structured 4-week cohort for 10–20 participants per organization. Courses run as group sessions (1–2 hours each) and can be delivered in-person at your facility or via tablet/computer.

Week 1
How Money Actually Works
Banking system mechanics, credit, debt cycles, and why traditional systems create barriers for people rebuilding from zero.
Week 2
Digital Wallets & Financial Control
Setting up a secure digital wallet. Understanding ownership vs. custody. How to hold and protect money without a bank account.
Week 3
Smart Financial Decisions
Transaction awareness, fee avoidance, scheduled payments, and avoiding predatory financial products like payday loans.
Week 4
Building a Financial Foundation
Long-term planning, community economics, how to earn and build value in the digital economy, and next steps after the program.

Funding This Program at Your Organization

If your organization wants to fund an ongoing or expanded partnership, the following federal and private grant programs are well-suited to cover this type of financial literacy programming:

Federal — DOJ / Dept. of Education
Second Chance Pell Grant Program
Covers education programs for incarcerated individuals. Financial literacy and digital economy courses qualify as eligible workforce development content.
Federal — HUD
Continuum of Care (CoC) Program
Funds shelter operations and support services including workforce readiness. Financial education aligns with housing stability outcomes required by CoC reports.
State / Local
Reentry Task Force Grants
Most states fund reentry programs independently. Digital skills and financial literacy components significantly strengthen grant applications.
Private Foundation
JPMorgan Chase / MacArthur / Ford Foundation
All three actively fund community financial empowerment programs. Partnership with Kenostod strengthens your application narrative and outcomes documentation.
