💧 What is FALP?

Flash Arbitrage Loan Pools (FALP) are collective liquidity pools where multiple users combine their KENO tokens. This pooled capital is then available for traders to borrow for flash arbitrage trades.

The Core Concept

Think of FALP like a cooperative bank account. Instead of your money sitting idle in a savings account earning 0.5% APY, you deposit it into a pool where it's actively lent out for arbitrage trades — earning you a share of the 0.05% fee from every trade.

⚙️ Pool Mechanics

Understanding how pools work is essential for maximizing your returns.

How Deposits Work

  • Select a Pool
    Choose from existing pools based on your risk tolerance and desired lock period. Each pool has different characteristics.
  • Deposit KENO
    Transfer KENO to the pool smart contract. You receive LP (Liquidity Provider) tokens representing your share.
  • Earn Continuously
    Every time a trader borrows from your pool, you earn a proportional share of the 0.05% liquidity provider fee.
  • Withdraw + Profits
    After the lock period, withdraw your original deposit plus accumulated profits.

LP Token Math

When you deposit into a pool, you receive LP tokens that represent your share:

Your LP Tokens = (Your Deposit / Total Pool) × Total LP Supply

📈 Example: Calculating Your Share

Pool Stats:

  • Total Pool: 50,000 KENO
  • Total LP Tokens: 50,000
  • Your Deposit: 5,000 KENO

Your LP Tokens: (5,000 / 50,000) × 50,000 = 5,000 LP

Your Share: 10% of all pool profits

Utilization Rate

Not all pool capital is borrowed at all times. The utilization rate measures how actively a pool's capital is being used.

Utilization Rate = (Capital Borrowed / Total Pool Size) × 100%

Higher utilization = more fees generated = higher returns. The best pools typically see 60-80% utilization.

⚠️ Understanding "Risk" in FALP

Risk in FALP is different from FAL. You don't lose money from failed arbitrage trades — those simply revert. Risk factors include:

  • Smart contract risk: Bug in pool code (mitigated by audits)
  • Opportunity cost: Locked capital could have been used elsewhere
  • Lower-than-expected returns: If utilization is low, fees are lower

Calculating Your Returns

Your Daily Profit = Pool Daily Volume × 0.0005 × Your Share %

💡 Lock Period Strategy

If you don't need the capital, longer locks significantly boost returns. A 365-day lock doubles your effective returns compared to flexible withdrawals. However, only lock what you can afford to have inaccessible.

Early Withdrawal

If you need to withdraw before your lock period ends:

  • Penalty: Forfeit 50% of accrued bonus earnings
  • Base Earnings: You keep 100% of base fee earnings
  • Principal: Always returned in full

🏗️ Creating Your Own Pool

Advanced users can create their own FALP pools with custom parameters.

Pool Creation Requirements

  • Graduate Status
    Must have completed all 21 courses to create pools. This ensures pool creators understand the system fully.

🎯 Investment Strategies

Maximize your FALP returns with these proven strategies.

Strategy 1: The Ladder

Spread deposits across multiple lock periods for flexibility + bonus earnings.

✅ Implementation

  • 25% in Flexible (emergency access)
  • 25% in 30-day lock
  • 25% in 90-day lock
  • 25% in 365-day lock

Result: Balanced liquidity with boosted average returns.

Strategy 2: Risk Tiering

Diversify across risk levels to balance returns and safety.

✅ Implementation

  • 50% in Conservative pools (stable base)
  • 35% in Balanced pools (growth)
  • 15% in Aggressive pools (upside potential)

Result: Blended returns with downside protection.

Strategy 3: Compound Snowball

Reinvest all earnings monthly to accelerate growth.

✅ Implementation

  • Start with maximum affordable deposit
  • Set reminder to reinvest earnings every 30 days
  • Never withdraw principal for at least 1 year

Result: Exponential growth through compounding.

Strategy 4: New Pool First Mover

Join new pools early to capture higher initial yields.

✅ Implementation

  • Monitor for new pool announcements
  • Evaluate pool creator reputation
  • Deposit quickly but don't overcommit

Result: Premium yields before pools become saturated.
