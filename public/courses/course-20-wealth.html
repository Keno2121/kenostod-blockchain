📊 Asset Classes Deep Dive

Different assets behave differently. Understanding these behaviors is crucial for building a portfolio that matches your goals and risk tolerance.

Traditional Asset Classes

Equities (Stocks)

Ownership shares in companies. Historically the highest-returning asset class over long periods, but with significant volatility.

  • Expected Return: 8-12% annually over long periods
  • Risk: High - Can drop 30-50% in bear markets
  • Best For: Long-term growth (10+ year horizon)

Fixed Income (Bonds)

Loans to governments or corporations that pay regular interest.

  • Expected Return: 4-6% annually
  • Risk: Low to Moderate
  • Best For: Income generation, portfolio stability

Real Estate

Physical property or REITs (Real Estate Investment Trusts).

  • Expected Return: 7-10% (appreciation + rental income)
  • Risk: Moderate - Illiquid, local market dependent
  • Best For: Income + inflation hedge

Digital Asset Classes

Cryptocurrency (BTC, ETH)

Decentralized digital currencies with finite supplies.

  • Expected Return: Highly variable (historically 100%+ in bull years, -80% in bear years)
  • Risk: Very High - Extreme volatility
  • Best For: High-risk/high-reward allocation (5-15% of portfolio)

KENO Token

Knowledge Yield Token with unique properties.

  • Expected Return: Platform growth dependent + FAL income potential
  • Risk: Moderate-High (early-stage but backed by real utility)
  • Best For: Combining education rewards with passive income

RVT NFTs

Residual Value Tokens providing perpetual royalty income.

  • Expected Return: Variable based on tier and platform activity
  • Risk: Moderate - Platform dependent
  • Best For: Passive income stream building

🎯 Strategic Asset Allocation

Asset allocation is the most important investment decision you'll make. Studies show it determines 90%+ of portfolio returns over time — more than individual security selection.

Factors to Consider

  • Time Horizon
    How long until you need the money? Longer horizons allow more risk-taking because you can ride out volatility.
  • Risk Tolerance
    How would you react to a 30% portfolio drop? If you'd panic-sell, you need a more conservative allocation.
  • Income Needs
    Do you need regular cash flow from investments? This favors bonds, dividend stocks, and RVTs.
  • Goals
    Retirement? Home purchase? Education? Different goals require different strategies.

⚖️ Rebalancing Mastery

Over time, different assets grow at different rates, causing your allocation to drift from targets. Rebalancing brings it back in line.

Why Rebalancing Matters

📈 Drift Example

Starting Portfolio (January):

  • Stocks: 60% ($60,000)
  • Bonds: 30% ($30,000)
  • Crypto: 10% ($10,000)

After Crypto Bull Run (December):

Stocks up 10%, Bonds up 4%, Crypto up 150%

  • Stocks: $66,000 (47% of portfolio)
  • Bonds: $31,200 (22% of portfolio)
  • Crypto: $25,000 (18% of portfolio) ← Now double target!

Total: $122,200 — Risk profile has shifted significantly

Rebalancing Methods

  • Calendar Rebalancing
    Review and rebalance at set intervals (quarterly, annually). Simple and disciplined.
  • Threshold Rebalancing
    Rebalance when any asset drifts more than 5% from target. More responsive to market moves.
  • Cash Flow Rebalancing
    Direct new investments to underweight assets instead of selling overweight ones. Tax-efficient.

Tax-Smart Rebalancing

Selling winners triggers capital gains taxes. Strategies to minimize this:

  • Rebalance inside tax-advantaged accounts (401k, IRA) first
  • Use new contributions to buy underweight assets
  • Harvest losses to offset gains when selling
  • For crypto: Consider KENO→FALP moves (no sale, just staking)
