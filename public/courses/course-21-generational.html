🏛️ Building a Legacy

Generational wealth is assets passed down from one generation to the next. It's not just about money — it's about providing opportunities, security, and a foundation for your descendants to build upon.

The 3-Generation Problem

Studies show a troubling pattern:

  • 70% of wealth is lost by the second generation
  • 90% of wealth is lost by the third generation

The phrase "shirtsleeves to shirtsleeves in three generations" describes this cycle. The first generation builds wealth through hard work. The second generation, having witnessed that work, maintains it. The third generation, having never struggled, often loses it.

📜 Estate Planning Fundamentals

Estate planning ensures your assets go where you want them to — efficiently and according to your wishes.

Essential Documents

  • Will (Last Will and Testament)
    Legal document specifying who receives your assets. Without one, state law decides — often not what you'd want. Everyone needs a will.
  • Living Trust (Revocable Trust)
    Assets transferred to a trust while you're alive. Avoids probate (expensive court process), provides privacy, and allows for complex distribution rules.
  • Power of Attorney
    Designates someone to make financial decisions if you're incapacitated. Critical for unexpected situations.
  • Healthcare Directive
    Specifies your medical wishes if you can't communicate them. Reduces family burden during difficult times.

Trust Structures for Wealth Transfer

Trust Type Key Feature Best For
Revocable Living Trust Can be changed anytime General estate planning
Irrevocable Trust Cannot be changed, tax benefits Asset protection, large estates
Generation-Skipping Trust Passes to grandchildren Multi-generational wealth
Charitable Trust Benefits charity + heirs Tax reduction + philanthropy

⚠️ Start Now

Most people think estate planning is for the old or wealthy. Wrong. Anyone with assets (including crypto) needs basic estate planning. Accidents happen at any age. Get at least a will and beneficiary designations set up.

🔐 Cryptocurrency Inheritance

Crypto poses unique challenges for inheritance. If private keys are lost, the assets are lost forever. An estimated $140 billion in Bitcoin is permanently inaccessible due to lost keys.

The Inheritance Challenge

Traditional assets (bank accounts, stocks) have institutions that can transfer ownership upon death. Cryptocurrency is self-custodied — there's no bank to call. If your heirs don't have access to your keys, they can't access your crypto.

Solutions for Crypto Inheritance

  • Documentation
    Maintain an encrypted document listing all crypto holdings, wallet addresses, and exchange accounts. Store the decryption key separately from the document itself.
  • Multi-Signature Wallets
    Create wallets requiring multiple signatures. You hold 2 of 3 keys; the third goes to a trusted person or is held in escrow for heirs.
  • Social Recovery (Kenostod)
    Kenostod's guardian system allows you to designate trusted people who can collectively restore access to your wallet. No single guardian can access your funds alone.
  • Dead Man's Switch
    Services that automatically release key information if you don't check in for a specified period (e.g., 6 months without login triggers release to heirs).
  • Hardware Wallet + Instructions
    Store hardware wallet in a safe. Include detailed instructions for recovery. Make sure heirs know the safe location and combination.
