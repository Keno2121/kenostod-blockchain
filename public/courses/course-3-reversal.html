💸 The $40 Billion Problem

Traditional cryptocurrencies like Bitcoin and Ethereum have a major flaw: transactions are completely irreversible. Once you hit send, there's no going back.

Real-World Consequences

  • Wrong Address: A single typo sends your life savings to the wrong wallet forever
  • Scam Victims: Phishing attacks steal funds with zero recourse
  • Fat Finger Errors: Accidentally sending 100 BTC instead of 1 BTC is permanent
  • Duress Situations: Forced transfers under threat cannot be stopped

⚠️ Industry Statistics

Over $40 billion in cryptocurrency has been permanently lost due to irreversible transaction errors, scams, and mistakes. This is a fundamental barrier to mainstream adoption.

💡 The Core Problem

Traditional blockchains were designed for "trustless" environments where you assume everyone might be malicious. But in the real world, most users are honest people who occasionally make mistakes. Current blockchain design punishes honest mistakes as harshly as it punishes fraud.

✅ Why 5 Minutes?

Five minutes is long enough to catch mistakes (you usually realize within seconds) but short enough to maintain practical transaction finality for merchants and exchanges.

⚙️ How It Works Technically

Transaction States

  • Pending: Transaction is in mempool, within 5-minute window, CAN be reversed
  • Confirmed: Past 5-minute window, included in a block, CANNOT be reversed
  • Reversed: Sender cancelled within window, funds returned, transaction voided

The Reversal Process

  • Sender Initiates: Only the original sender can request reversal
  • Signature Verification: System verifies sender's digital signature matches original transaction
  • Time Check: Confirms transaction is still within 5-minute window
  • UTXO Restoration: Original UTXOs are unlocked and returned to sender
  • Transaction Voided: Pending transaction is removed from mempool

💡 Recipient Notification

Recipients see incoming transactions as "Pending (5:00)" with a countdown. This transparency means they know not to deliver goods/services until the timer expires. Smart merchants wait for confirmation!

🔐 Security Measures

The reversal system includes multiple safeguards to prevent abuse:

Anti-Abuse Protections

  • Sender-Only Reversal: Only the cryptographic owner of the sending wallet can reverse
  • One Reversal Per TX: A transaction can only be reversed once — no "reversal wars"
  • Strict Time Enforcement: Even 1 second past 5 minutes = permanent, no exceptions
  • Full Audit Trail: All reversals are logged on-chain for transparency

⚠️ Merchant Best Practices

Merchants should ALWAYS wait for the 5-minute window to expire before delivering goods or services. This is similar to waiting for credit card authorization — prudent business practice, not a system flaw.

When Reversal is NOT Available

  • High-Value Verified: Transactions over 10,000 KENO with verified recipient
  • Exchange Deposits: Deposits to verified exchange addresses
  • Smart Contract Calls: Interactions with deployed contracts
