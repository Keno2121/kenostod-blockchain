🔑 The Lost Key Problem

In traditional cryptocurrency, losing your private key or seed phrase means losing access to your funds forever. An estimated 20% of all Bitcoin (worth billions) is permanently inaccessible due to lost keys.

⚠️ The Reality

"Not your keys, not your coins" is a common crypto mantra. But the flip side is equally true: "Lose your keys, lose your coins." This creates an impossible choice between security and recoverability.

Problems with Seed Phrases

  • Easy to Lose: Paper gets damaged, files get deleted
  • Easy to Steal: Anyone who finds your seed phrase owns your funds
  • No Recovery: Once lost, there's no way to recover access
  • Inheritance Issues: How do you pass crypto to heirs securely?

👥 The Guardian System

Kenostod's Social Recovery allows you to designate trusted guardians — friends, family, or institutions who can help you recover your wallet without ever having access to your funds.

Figure: Guardian network protecting your wallet

How Guardians Work

  • Designate Guardians: Choose 3-7 trusted people/entities
  • Set Threshold: Decide how many must approve (e.g., 3 of 5)
  • No Key Access: Guardians CANNOT access your funds normally
  • Recovery Only: They can only approve key replacement requests

💡 Threshold Cryptography

Using "m of n" thresholds (like 3 of 5), no single guardian can recover your wallet alone. This prevents both accidents and malicious guardians from compromising your funds.

🔄 The Recovery Process

  • Initiate Recovery: You (or your new device) requests wallet recovery
  • Contact Guardians: System notifies your designated guardians
  • Guardian Verification: Guardians verify your identity (phone call, video, in person)
  • Approval Threshold: Once enough guardians approve, recovery proceeds
  • New Key Generation: New private key is created, old one is invalidated
  • Access Restored: You regain control of your wallet with new credentials

✅ Time Lock Protection

There's a 48-hour delay between guardian approval and actual recovery. This gives you time to notice and cancel a fraudulent recovery attempt.

🛡️ Security Design

Anti-Collusion Measures

  • Diverse Guardians: Choose guardians who don't know each other
  • Geographic Distribution: Guardians in different locations
  • Mixed Types: Combine friends, family, and institutional guardians
  • Regular Rotation: Update guardians periodically

⚠️ Guardian Selection Tips

Never choose guardians who could benefit from stealing your funds or who are likely to collude. Ideally, your guardians shouldn't know who the other guardians are.
