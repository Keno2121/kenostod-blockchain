🏛️ What is a DAO?

A Decentralized Autonomous Organization (DAO) is an organization governed by code and community votes rather than a central authority. Kenostod is governed as a DAO where KENO holders vote on network decisions.

Figure: How proposals flow through the DAO governance process

💡 Decentralized Decision Making

Unlike traditional companies where executives make decisions, DAOs let token holders collectively decide the future. Every KENO holder has a voice proportional to their stake.

🗳️ Token-Weighted Voting

Your voting power in Kenostod governance is determined by how many KENO tokens you hold. This ensures those with the most stake in the network have the most say.

How Voting Power Works

  • 1 KENO = 1 Vote: Each token gives you one vote
  • Reputation Bonus: High reputation multiplies your voting power
  • Time Lock Bonus: Tokens locked longer get more voting weight

Example Vote Result

Proposal: Reduce Transaction Fees by 50%

YES: 680,000 KENO (68%)
NO: 320,000 KENO (32%)

✅ Proposal PASSED

📝 Creating Proposals

Any KENO holder with sufficient tokens can create proposals for the community to vote on.

What Can Be Proposed?

  • Fee Changes: Adjust transaction fee percentages
  • Mining Rewards: Modify block rewards and distribution
  • Network Parameters: Block size, confirmation times, etc.
  • Treasury Spending: Fund development, marketing, grants
  • Protocol Upgrades: New features and improvements

✅ Proposal Requirements

To create a proposal, you need at least 1,000 KENO staked. Proposals have a 7-day voting period. A 60% supermajority with 10% quorum is required to pass.
